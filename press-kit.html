Sagelight Studio Launches SageBills — The Privacy-First Utility Tracker Built for European Households

ROMANIA, April 2026 — Sagelight Studio

ROMANIA — Sagelight Studio today announced the launch of SageBills, a free utility bill tracking application for iPhone and Mac designed specifically for European households. SageBills addresses a critical gap in the personal finance market: while over 27 million Central and Eastern European (CEE) households manage multiple utility contracts across electricity, gas, water, internet, and heating, no dedicated tool has existed to track, analyze, and predict these recurring expenses — until now.

Available immediately on the App Store for iOS 17+ and macOS 14+, SageBills provides a private, offline-first experience built around the realities of European utility billing: regional providers, local currencies, paper invoices, and the unpredictable cost swings characteristic of CEE energy markets.

Addressing a Market Ignored by Western Apps

Popular budgeting applications such as Mint, YNAB, and Spendee are designed primarily for North American or Western European markets, offering bank integrations and subscription tracking that do not map to Eastern European utility structures. SageBills is built from the ground up for the CEE context: 27 EU countries, 39 pre-loaded providers, 40+ bill categories, and 12+ supported currencies.

Privacy as Architecture, Not Policy

Unlike most financial apps, SageBills collects no user data whatsoever. The application contains zero third-party SDKs, requires no account or email registration, and operates entirely on-device. All bill data is stored locally using Apple's SwiftData framework with NSFileProtectionComplete encryption. No analytics, telemetry, or advertising identifiers of any kind are present in the codebase.

"Privacy in financial apps is usually a checkbox — a policy document. We made it an architectural constraint," said the founder of Sagelight Studio. "SageBills cannot leak your data because it never touches our servers. That's not a feature; it's the foundation."

On-Device Intelligence

SageBills includes AI-powered bill predictions and anomaly detection that run entirely on-device using Core ML. The app can identify unusual spikes in utility costs, forecast upcoming bills based on historical patterns, and surface savings opportunities — all without transmitting any data to external servers. OCR scanning for paper invoices is also processed locally.

Key Features
  • Bill tracking dashboard with monthly and annual summaries
  • Calendar heatmap visualizing spending across the year
  • On-device AI predictions for upcoming bills and cost anomaly alerts
  • OCR document scanning for paper invoices (fully local)
  • 39 pre-loaded utility providers across 27 EU countries
  • 12+ currency support including RON, HUF, CZK, PLN, and more
  • Savings goals and budget tracking per utility category
  • iCloud sync across iPhone and Mac (zero Sagelight servers involved)
Availability and Pricing

SageBills is available now on the App Store at no cost. There is no freemium model, no subscription tier, no advertising, and no in-app purchases. The app is supported solely by the developer's commitment to building honest software.

About Sagelight Studio

Sagelight Studio is an independent software studio based in Romania, European Union, founded by a solo developer with a focus on privacy-respecting productivity and personal finance tools for European users. Current apps include SageBills (utility bill tracking), SageMeet (AI meeting recorder), and SageDocs (encrypted document vault). Editorial Angles

Key Talking Points

01 / The CEE Gap
Most Finance Apps Ignore European Utility Patterns
Western budgeting tools are built around bank feeds, credit card tracking, and subscription management. CEE households pay 5–8 separate utility bills monthly, often in cash or via local bank transfer, with providers that Mint or YNAB have never heard of. SageBills is the first app built for this reality.
02 / Privacy as a Feature
Zero SDKs, Zero Tracking, Zero Accounts
SageBills collects nothing. No analytics framework, no advertising SDK, no crash reporter, no account system. The app is structurally incapable of leaking financial data because it never transmits any. In an era of financial data breaches, this is the only responsible architecture for a bill-tracking app.
03 / AI That Works Offline
Predictions and Anomaly Detection Run On-Device
Bill cost predictions, seasonal forecasting, and anomaly alerts are powered by Core ML and run entirely on the user's device. No data is sent to OpenAI, no API key is required, and the features work in airplane mode. This is AI built the right way for sensitive financial data.
04 / Built for Real Bills
39 Providers Pre-Loaded, OCR for Local Invoices
SageBills ships with 39 utility providers across 27 EU countries, covering electricity, gas, water, internet, heating, and waste. For providers not in the database, on-device OCR scanning reads paper invoices directly. No manual data entry for the majority of European users.
05 / Free With No Catch
No Freemium, No Ads, No Data Monetization
SageBills is free — completely. No premium tier locks away useful features. No ads appear between your bill entries. The developer does not sell or monetize user data (and couldn't, because none is collected). The business model is honest software: build something good, charge fairly, and respect users.
06 / Made in Europe
Romanian Indie Developer, GDPR-Native
Sagelight Studio is a Romanian indie developer building for the European market from within it. GDPR compliance is not a legal afterthought — it is the starting architecture. SageBills was designed to be fully GDPR compliant even before a single line of code was written, because the developer operates under GDPR themselves.

FAQ for Journalists

SageBills is a free utility bill tracking application for iPhone and Mac, built specifically for European households. It lets users log, categorize, and analyze recurring utility expenses — electricity, gas, water, internet, heating, and more — using a fully local, privacy-first architecture. The app includes AI-powered predictions, OCR invoice scanning, a calendar heatmap, and savings goal tracking, all without requiring an account or transmitting any data off-device.

Currently, SageBills is supported directly by the developer as part of building a portfolio of honest, privacy-respecting apps. There are no ads, no in-app purchases, and no data monetization of any kind. The long-term model for Sagelight Studio is building a suite of apps that users trust enough to pay for directly — either as one-time purchases or optional support tiers. For now, SageBills is genuinely free with no hidden costs.

SageBills is purpose-built for utility bill tracking rather than general budgeting. The key differences are:

European-first: 39 pre-loaded EU providers, regional currencies, and bill structures that Western apps don't support. No bank connection required: Most budgeting apps require linking your bank account. SageBills is entirely manual and local. Zero accounts: No sign-up, no email, no cloud account. On-device AI: Bill predictions without any data leaving the device. Free, truly: No freemium walls hiding essential features.

SageBills uses Apple's Core ML framework to run prediction models directly on the user's device. The models analyze historical bill data stored locally — amounts, dates, seasonal patterns, and provider behavior — to forecast upcoming bills and flag anomalies (e.g., an electricity bill that is 40% higher than the seasonal average). No data is sent to any external API. The predictions improve as the user logs more bills over time, all without any server involvement.

No. SageBills does not connect to bank accounts, payment systems, or any financial institution. All bill data is entered manually by the user (or captured via OCR from paper invoices). This is a deliberate design decision: connecting to a bank account requires trusting a third-party service with sensitive credentials, which contradicts SageBills' privacy-first architecture.

None. SageBills collects no data. There are no analytics SDKs, no telemetry, no crash reporters, no advertising identifiers, and no usage tracking of any kind. The app is built entirely with Apple's first-party frameworks. The only data that exists is the bill data you enter, stored locally on your device under NSFileProtectionComplete encryption. Sagelight Studio has no technical ability to access it, because it never leaves your device.

SageBills ships with 39 pre-loaded utility providers across all 27 EU member states, with particular depth in Central and Eastern European markets. Pre-loaded providers cover electricity, natural gas, water, internet/telecom, and district heating. Support spans 12+ currencies including EUR, RON, HUF, CZK, PLN, BGN, HRK, and others. Users can also add any custom provider for bills outside the pre-loaded database.

Not currently. SageBills is an iOS and macOS application built with SwiftUI, SwiftData, and Apple's Core ML framework. An Android version would require a significant rebuild from the ground up. There is no announced timeline for Android support. The developer is focused on delivering the best possible iOS/macOS experience first.
